例句 (EN/CN)
excavate v. /ˈek.sə.veɪt/
To dig carefully to uncover archaeological remains
挖掘，發掘（考古遺址）
"He returned from a summer spent excavating a Viking site in southern Greenland."
他從格陵蘭南部一個夏季的維京遺址發掘工作歸來。
midden n. /ˈmɪd.ən/
A trash heap or refuse pile, especially from ancient settlements
（古代聚居地的）垃圾堆，廢棄物堆
"The horse was found in a midden, or trash heap, raising questions about why it was discarded."
這匹馬是在一個垃圾堆中發現的，引發了關於它為何被丟棄的疑問。
fjord n. /fjɔːrd/
A long, narrow inlet with steep sides, created by glacial erosion
峽灣（冰川侵蝕形成的狹長海灣）
"Settlers clung to narrow meadows between the sea and the great ice sheet in the serpentine fjords of southwestern Greenland."
定居者依附於格陵蘭西南部蜿蜒峽灣中介於海洋同巨大冰蓋之間的狹窄草地。
extinct adj. /ɪkˈstɪŋkt/
No longer existing; having died out completely
滅絕的，絕跡的
"The subtext is, they all went extinct—the Norse colonies in Greenland went dark and their citizens disappeared."
言下之意是，他們都滅絕了——格陵蘭的諾爾斯殖民地陷入沉寂，居民消失了。
relic n. /ˈrel.ɪk/
An object surviving from an earlier time, especially one of historical interest
遺物，殘留物（尤指有歷史價值的）
"The ruins were the relics of people who hadn't survived in the hard, cold fjords."
這些廢墟是未能在寒冷艱苦的峽灣中倖存下來的人們的遺物。
outlaw n. /ˈaʊt.lɔː/
A person who has broken the law, especially one who remains at large
逃犯，亡命之徒
"They were led by an outlaw named Erik Thorvaldsson, better known as Erik the Red."
他們由一名名叫埃里克·索瓦爾德松的逃犯帶領，他更為人熟知的名字是「紅髮埃里克」。
exile n. /ˈek.saɪl/
The state of being barred from one's native country, typically for political or punitive reasons
流放，放逐
"Thorvaldsson had 'discovered' the island while serving a sentence of exile for murder."
索瓦爾德松在因謀殺罪服流放刑期間「發現」了這個島嶼。
apocalypse n. /əˈpɒk.ə.lɪps/
A catastrophic event causing widespread destruction and final devastation
世界末日，大災難
"If the Greenlanders all died in some apocalypse—plague, say, or warfare—their cousins in Europe apparently failed to notice."
如果格陵蘭人都死於某場大災難——比如瘟疫或戰爭——他們在歐洲的親戚顯然沒有注意到。
compelling adj. /kəmˈpel.ɪŋ/
Evoking interest, attention, or admiration in a powerfully irresistible way
令人信服的，引人入勝的
"Archaeologists are assembling a compelling new theory that explains their fate—one that links the end of their world with ours."
考古學家正在匯集一個令人信服的新理論來解釋他們的命運——這個理論將他們世界的終結與我們的世界聯繫起來。
heathland n. /ˈhiːθ.lənd/
An area of open uncultivated land, typically with low-growing shrubs
荒野，石楠叢生的荒地
"The Norse carried their culture from the fjords and heathlands of Norway, Denmark, and Sweden to remote outposts."
諾爾斯人將他們的文化從挪威、丹麥同瑞典的峽灣同荒野帶到了偏遠的前哨站。
