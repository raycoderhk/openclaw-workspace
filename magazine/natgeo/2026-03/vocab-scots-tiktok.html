🎷 Can TikTok Resurrect Scots?

Vocabulary & Quiz | 詞彙與測驗

📖 Read Original Article →

📄 Article Excerpt | 文章摘錄 (Proof of Real Content)

📍 First 100 Words | 首 100 字
When "hurkle-durkle" went viral on social media a couple of years ago, most of the people using the phrase had only a faint idea what language it came from—but not Len Pennie. Pennie is a poet and Scots language influencer with more than a million followers on Instagram, TikTok, and other platforms. Originally from just outside Glasgow, the 26-year-old appreciates that people connected with hurkle-durkle as a fun, funny-sounding way to say "lounge in bed when one should be up and about"—just like how the cozy, convivial concept of hygge, from Danish and Norwegian, entered the zeitgeist years before.
📍 Last 100 Words | 尾 100 字
...while battling for the hearts and minds (or, perhaps, the tongues and ears) of non-Scots speakers is a younger generation of creatives—singers, writers, social media natives, and others—reviving and reinventing the language for the 21st century. In their 20s and 30s, hailing from all over the country, they're bringing Scots into TikToks and text messages, pop music covers and poetry. "This has just all come out of nowhere," says Pennie of her own trajectory, as she steps out of the BBC Scotland studios in Glasgow, where she hosts a four-times-weekly, Scots-inflected radio show on arts and culture.

📝 Article Summary | 文章摘要

Scots language influencer Len Pennie and a younger generation of creatives are using TikTok and social media to revive the Scots language, which has been spoken by 1.5 million people but historically stigmatized. Despite being granted official status alongside Gaelic, Scots faces challenges from viral commodification and the need for standardization.

蘇格蘭語影響者 Len Pennie 同新一代創意人士正利用 TikTok 同社交媒體復興蘇格蘭語，這種語言有 150 萬人使用但歷史上被污名化。雖然獲得了與蓋爾語同等的官方地位，蘇格蘭語仍面臨病毒式商品化同標準化需求的挑戰。

viral adj. /ˈvaɪ.rəl/
Spreading rapidly through social media or the internet
病毒式傳播的，迅速走紅的
"When 'hurkle-durkle' went viral on social media a couple of years ago, most people had only a faint idea what language it came from."
當「hurkle-durkle」幾年前喺社交媒體上病毒式傳播時，大多數人只係隱約知道佢來自邊種語言。
influencer n. /ˈɪn.flu.ən.sər/
A person with the ability to influence potential buyers, especially on social media
影響者，網紅（尤指社交媒體上）
"Pennie is a poet and Scots language influencer with more than a million followers on Instagram, TikTok, and other platforms."
Pennie 係一位詩人同蘇格蘭語影響者，喺 Instagram、TikTok 等同其他平台上有超過一百萬粉絲。
convivial adj. /kənˈvɪv.i.əl/
Friendly, lively, and enjoyable; creating a festive atmosphere
歡暢的，愉快的，營造節日氣氛的
"People connected with hurkle-durkle like the cozy, convivial concept of hygge, from Danish and Norwegian."
人們喜歡 hurkle-durkle，就像丹麥語同挪威語中舒適歡暢嘅 hygge 概念一樣。
zeitgeist n. /ˈtsaɪt.ɡaɪst/
The defining spirit or mood of a particular period of history
時代精神，時代潮流
"Hygge entered the zeitgeist years before hurkle-durkle went viral."
Hygge 喺 hurkle-durkle 病毒式傳播之前幾年就進入了時代潮流。
stigma n. /ˈstɪɡ.mə/
A mark of disgrace associated with a particular circumstance or quality
恥辱，污名
"Scots still carries a kind of hillbilly stigma, and within living memory, you could get corporal punishment for speaking it at school."
蘇格蘭語仍然帶有一種鄉巴佬嘅污名，喺在世記憶中，你喺學校講佢會受到體罰。
code-switch v. /ˈkəʊd ˌswɪtʃ/
To alternate between two or more languages or dialects in conversation
語碼轉換，切換語言
"Young people, like most Scots, code-switch on a daily basis between English and Scots."
年輕人，像大多數蘇格蘭人一樣，每日喺英語同蘇格蘭語之間語碼轉換。
commodification n. /kəˌmɒd.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən/
The process of turning something into a product to be bought and sold
商品化
"Some worry that viralities like hurkle-durkle continue a history of not taking the language seriously—a process of cutesy commodification packaged for tourists."
有些人擔心好像 hurkle-durkle 嘅病毒式傳播延續了唔認真對待語言嘅歷史——一種為遊客包裝嘅可愛商品化過程。
dialect n. /ˈdaɪ.ə.lekt/
A particular form of a language specific to a region or social group
方言，地方話
"The Doric dialect of the Northeast and Aberdeenshire is different to the Scots of Ayrshire and Galloway."
東北部同阿伯丁郡嘅多立克方言同艾爾郡同加洛韋嘅蘇格蘭語唔同。
trajectory n. /trəˈdʒek.tər.i/
The path or course of development
軌跡，發展歷程
"'This has just all come out of nowhere,' says Pennie of her own trajectory, from therapist's advice to award-winning poet."
「呢一切都係突然發生嘅，」Pennie 談到自己嘅發展歷程，從治療師嘅建議到獲獎詩人。
ambassador n. /æmˈbæs.ə.dər/
A person who represents or promotes a particular activity or ideal
大使，推廣者
"Shane Strachan acts as a roving ambassador for the Scots language, visiting hospitals and book festivals."
Shane Strachan 擔任蘇格蘭語嘅巡迴大使，探訪醫院同書展。
