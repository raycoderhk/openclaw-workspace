🕷️ The Quest for Spider Silk

Vocabulary & Quiz | 詞彙與測驗

📖 Read Original Article →

📄 Article Excerpt | 文章摘錄 (Proof of Real Content)

📍 First 100 Words | 首 100 字
Somewhere in Michigan, 10,000 silkworms are spinning the future of supermaterials. They labor in the thick air of a warm, humid warehouse, pulling a sticky white strand from a gland in their face and weaving it into a cocoon the size of a grape. Since they were first domesticated in China thousands of years ago, their silk has been used to make the world's finest fabric. But these silkworms aren't like the millions that came before. They are spinning spider silk, or something close to it.
📍 Last 100 Words | 尾 100 字
...efforts to create spider proteins without the spider began in earnest in the 1990s, according to molecular biologist Randy Lewis, then at the University of Wyoming. At first, he and his team eyed E. coli as a potential host. Their thought process: If you could engineer the bacteria to produce spidroins as part of their regular metabolism, you could cultivate them in a fermentation tank, as if you were brewing beer, then separate the spidroins from the mix, spin them into fibers, and voilà: spider silk. That failed.

📝 Article Summary | 文章摘要

Scientists have genetically engineered silkworms to produce spider silk—a material five times stronger than steel. After decades of failed attempts using bacteria, goats, and alfalfa, companies like Kraig Biocraft are now successfully producing "supersilk" at scale, but face the challenge of finding practical applications.

科學家通過基因工程改造蠶寶寶生產蜘蛛絲——一種強度係鋼五倍嘅材料。經過數十年使用細菌、山羊同苜蓿嘅失敗嘗試，Kraig Biocraft 等公司而家成功大規模生產「超級絲」，但面臨尋找實際應用嘅挑戰。

To tame animals or plants for human use

"Since they were first domesticated in China thousands of years ago, silkworms have been used to make the world's finest fabric."
自從幾千年前喺中國首次被馴化以來，蠶寶寶一直被用於製造世界上最精細嘅織物。
transgenic adj. /trænzˈdʒen.ɪk/
Containing genes from another organism
轉基因的
"At Soochow University, a single fiber of supersilk produced by a transgenic silkworm is stress tested."
喺蘇州大學，一條由轉基因蠶寶寶生產嘅超級絲纖維接受應力測試。
spidroin n. /ˈspɪd.roʊ.ɪn/
The protein that makes up spider silk
蜘蛛絲蛋白
"Spider silk's capabilities derive from special proteins known as spidroins, composed of thousands of amino acids."
蜘蛛絲嘅能力來自稱為蜘蛛絲蛋白嘅特殊蛋白質，由數千個氨基酸組成。
hydrophobic adj. /ˌhaɪ.drəˈfəʊ.bɪk/
Repelling or failing to mix with water
疏水的，防水的
"Spidroins mix both positive and negative electrical charges as well as hydrophobic and hydrophilic sections."
蜘蛛絲蛋白混合了正負電荷以及疏水同親水部分。
commercialize v. /kəˈmɜː.ʃəl.aɪz/
To manage something to make a profit
商業化，營利化
"The first attempt to commercialize spider silk didn't start until the late 1800s, when Jesuit missionaries in Madagascar tried it."
首次嘗試商業化蜘蛛絲直到 19 世紀後期先開始，當時馬達加斯加嘅耶穌會傳教士嘗試咗。
fermentation n. /ˌfɜː.menˈteɪ.ʃən/
The process of breaking down substances using bacteria or yeast
發酵
"Scientists thought they could cultivate bacteria in a fermentation tank, as if brewing beer, to produce spider silk."
科學家認為佢哋可以喺發酵罐中培養細菌，好像釀啤酒一樣，來生產蜘蛛絲。
metabolism n. /məˈtæb.ə.lɪ.zəm/
The chemical processes in an organism that maintain life
新陳代謝
"The idea was to engineer bacteria to produce spidroins as part of their regular metabolism."
想法係改造細菌，使佢哋將蜘蛛絲蛋白作為正常新陳代謝的一部分來生產。
synthetic adj. /sɪnˈθet.ɪk/
Made by chemical synthesis, especially to imitate a natural product
合成的，人造的
"While synthetic fibers like Kevlar and graphene have revolutionized industries, they're dogged by their artificiality."
雖然像 Kevlar 同石墨烯嘅合成纖維徹底改變了行業，但佢哋被人造性所困擾。
analog n. /ˈæn.ə.lɒɡ/
A thing comparable to something else
類似物，類比
"Companies have made breakthroughs in manufacturing spider silk, or a very close analog of it."
公司喺生產蜘蛛絲或其非常接近嘅類似物方面取得了突破。
hype n. /haɪp/
Extravagant or intensive publicity or promotion
炒作，大肆宣傳
